<p><p>The chlorine market comprises various production processes, each with distinct characteristics. The Mercury Cell Process utilizes mercury electrodes but raises environmental concerns due to toxicity. The Membrane Cell Process is more environmentally friendly, using ion-exchange membranes for higher purity and lower energy consumption. The Diaphragm Process employs a porous diaphragm to separate chlorine and caustic soda, resulting in lower purity levels. Other methods include the electrolysis of salt brine, utilizing different technologies, but these three dominate the market landscape.</p></p>
