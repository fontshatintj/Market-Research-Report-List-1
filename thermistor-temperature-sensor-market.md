<p><p>A thermistor temperature sensor is a type of thermally sensitive resistor whose resistance varies significantly with temperature. These sensors are widely utilized in various applications, including consumer electronics, automotive systems, medical devices, and industrial processes, due to their high sensitivity and accurate temperature measurements. Thermistors are typically classified into two types: NTC (Negative Temperature Coefficient), which decreases in resistance with an increase in temperature, and PTC (Positive Temperature Coefficient), which exhibits an increase in resistance with rising temperature.</p><p>The Thermistor Temperature Sensor Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 11.8% during the forecast period. <p><p>The Thermistor Temperature Sensor market is primarily divided into two types: contact and non-contact thermistors. Contact thermistors require direct physical contact with the object being measured, providing accurate temperature readings, ideal for solids and liquids. In contrast, non-contact thermistors utilize infrared technology to measure temperature from a distance, making them suitable for moving objects or environments where contact is impractical. Both types serve various applications across industrial, medical, and environmental sectors, catering to diverse temperature measurement needs.</p></p>
