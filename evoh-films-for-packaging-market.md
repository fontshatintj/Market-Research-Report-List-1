<p><p>EVOH (ethylene vinyl alcohol) films are essential in packaging due to their excellent barrier properties against gases, making them ideal for food preservation. The market is segmented into blown and cast film types. Blown films are produced by extruding molten material through a circular die, resulting in high strength and thickness. In contrast, cast films are formed by cooling a thin layer of extruded material on a chill roll, offering a smooth surface and uniform thickness, suitable for flexible applications.</p></p>
