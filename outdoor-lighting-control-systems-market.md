<p><p>Outdoor lighting control systems are categorized into two primary technologies: wired and wireless. Wired technology involves physical connections through cables to control lighting fixtures, offering reliability and stability, especially in extensive installations. In contrast, wireless technology utilizes radio frequency or other wireless protocols for control, allowing for easier installation and flexibility in system reconfiguration. Both types enable enhanced energy efficiency, safety, and convenience, catering to various outdoor settings such as parks, streets, and commercial areas.</p></p>
