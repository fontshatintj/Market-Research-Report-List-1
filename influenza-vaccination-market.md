<p><p>The influenza vaccination market comprises three primary types: egg-based, cell culture, and recombinant vaccines. Egg-based vaccines, the most traditional method, use fertilized chicken eggs for virus propagation. Cell culture vaccines utilize mammalian cells, offering faster production and adaptability to different strains. Recombinant vaccines leverage genetic engineering to produce antigens in a lab setting, enhancing safety and effectiveness. Each type plays a vital role in public health, ensuring a diverse supply of vaccines to combat seasonal and pandemic influenza strains.</p></p>
