<p><p>The cheese ingredients market is divided into two primary types: natural and processed. Natural cheese, which includes varieties like Cheddar, Parmesan, Mozzarella, and Gouda, is made from milk through fermentation and aging, offering rich flavors and textures. Processed cheese, on the other hand, involves combining natural cheese with emulsifiers, oils, and other ingredients, resulting in a product with a longer shelf life and consistent melting properties. These categories cater to different culinary needs and consumer preferences, influencing production and consumption trends.</p></p>
